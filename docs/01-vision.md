# Vision & Strategy
## Lore — Wikipedia as a Social Experience

## 1. Executive Summary
Lore is a next-generation knowledge discovery platform that reimagines Wikipedia as an intelligent, personalized social media feed. Where platforms like X.com, Instagram, and TikTok compete for attention with noise, Lore competes with *signal* — surfacing humanity's most verified, deep knowledge in a format engineered for modern consumption.

> **One-liner:** Wikipedia, but it knows you — and it never wastes your time.

## 2. Problem Statement
### 2.1 The Knowledge Gap
- Wikipedia hosts 6.8M+ English articles — one of humanity's greatest intellectual achievements
- 99.97% of Wikipedia's knowledge is never discovered by casual users
- Wikipedia's interface hasn't meaningfully changed in 20 years
- Social media algorithms reward engagement over truth, novelty over depth

### 2.2 The Attention Economy Problem
| Platform | What it feeds you | Cost |
|----------|-------------------|------|
| TikTok | Dopamine spikes | Your attention span |
| Twitter/X | Hot takes + outrage | Your peace |
| Instagram | Curated lives | Your self-esteem |
| **Lore** | **Verified knowledge** | **Nothing. You gain.** |

### 2.3 What Xikipedia Proved — and Left Undone
The original Xikipedia (rebane2001) validated the concept: Wikipedia *can* work as a feed. But it left a canyon of unbuilt potential:
- No real algorithm (3-rule weighted random)
- No user identity or personalization
- No social sharing infrastructure
- No semantic understanding of content
- UI is a functional throwaway

## 3. Vision & Mission
**Vision:** The world's smartest feed. A place where scrolling makes you smarter.

**Mission:** Build an algorithmically-intelligent, beautifully-designed platform that surfaces Wikipedia's 6.8M articles in a personalized, social-native format — growing users' knowledge graphs as they consume.

## 4. Target Users
### Primary: The Curious Learner (25–38)
- Educated professional who's aware social media is rotting their attention
- Listens to podcasts, reads newsletters, identifies as "lifelong learner"
- Wants to feel like they're doing something useful when they scroll
- **Pain:** No platform rewards intellectual curiosity at scale

### Secondary: The Deep Diver (18–28)
- Student, researcher, or developer
- Falls into Wikipedia rabbit holes intentionally
- Wants better navigation across connected knowledge
- **Pain:** Wikipedia is flat — no feed, no discovery engine

### Tertiary: The Knowledge Sharer
- Creator, educator, journalist
- Wants to share interesting facts/history/science in a shareable format
- **Pain:** Sharing Wikipedia links is ugly and context-free

## 5. Core Value Proposition
```
For curious people who are tired of mindless scrolling,
Lore is a knowledge feed
that delivers personalized Wikipedia content through an intelligent algorithm,
unlike Twitter or TikTok
which optimizes for outrage and addiction instead of growth.
```
